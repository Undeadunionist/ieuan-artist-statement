Ieuan Holt

Artist Statement

In lanes last walked when Dragons flew,

in still summer skies, I take your hand in mine,

both laughing at a hill steeper than I remember

our breath, cast out fishing nets,

holding onto everything but water.

With a look, rest, and two more steps,

you synthesise the view:

hedgerows melting into molded forms,

distant trees,

fields flow and funnel into stone buildings

that in a blink,

return again as castles,

open nine till three.

Halfway down the lane,

hand still in mine, the moment kept on a page,

we talk of church water left for pilgrims

and how nice the gesture,

though we wouldn’t drink.

Out to our left, nestled beneath a tree familiar,

rests a dragon exhaling breath,

our only, much-needed breeze.

'Whitpot Hill' by Ieuan Holt

There are places that take hold of us. Long after we’ve left, they linger in our minds—shifting, changing, always calling us back. My work explores this pull, the way we experience landscapes not only as physical spaces, but as sites of memory, emotion, and transformation. We are the patchwork fields of all the places we have been—shaped by the terrains we’ve moved through, carrying them within us like maps of who we’ve been.

My project, Gone Outside, III, returns to the places that have shaped my way of seeing—St. Germans and the Midlands. Through painting and written reflections, I explore the tension between past and present, how memory distorts reality, and how places hold echoes of who we were. These landscapes exist between real and imagined, inviting viewers to step into their own memories and emotions.

My paintings are not just about looking, but about feeling—wandering through spaces where familiarity and change coexist, where the past is never quite gone, only shifting with us. By revisiting these landscapes, I seek to uncover the stories they hold and the ways they continue to shape us. Through my work, I aim to create spaces for reflection, connection, and the rediscovery of the magic that lingers in the familiar.
